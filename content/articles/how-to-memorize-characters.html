Learn components before full characters

Character memory becomes easier when students learn recurring parts first. Many characters share radicals and visual components that carry meaning or sound hints, a pattern native speakers rely on naturally.

Instead of treating every character as a completely new drawing, look for repeated building blocks.

Pair each character with a useful phrase

Memorization is stronger when connected to context. For each new character, write one short phrase that you would realistically use in conversation or writing.

  • Read the phrase aloud.
  • Type it once.
  • Handwrite it once with correct stroke order.

Review by spaced repetition

Review sessions should happen across increasing intervals, for example after 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, and 14 days. This pattern reduces forgetting and saves total study time.

Focus your energy on weak characters while briefly refreshing familiar ones.
