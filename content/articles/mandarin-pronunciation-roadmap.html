Start with initials, finals, and tone pairs

Mandarin pronunciation improves fastest when students separate sound training from grammar training. Spend focused time on initials and finals, then combine them into short drills.

Tone pairs are especially important. Practice common combinations such as first-third and second-fourth, then record your voice and compare it with a native sample.

Build a 15-minute daily speaking block

Keep the routine simple: 5 minutes of sound drills, 5 minutes of sentence repetition, and 5 minutes of free speaking with guided prompts.

  • Use short lines first, then increase sentence length.
  • Prioritize clarity over speed.
  • Repeat difficult words in meaningful phrases, not in isolation only.

Use feedback loops

Ask your tutor to mark only one or two key pronunciation priorities per lesson. Correcting everything at once is inefficient and demotivating.

With consistent targeted feedback, pronunciation usually becomes noticeably clearer after a few weeks.
